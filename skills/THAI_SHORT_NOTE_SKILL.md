# THAI_SHORT_NOTE_SKILL.md

## Purpose

This skill is for converting teaching-style documents into short notes in natural Thai.

The target output is not a summary in the abstract sense. It is a study note that helps Thai students review a topic quickly before reading, after reading, and before exams.

The writing should feel like it was made by a real Thai student who understands the topic well enough to simplify it clearly. It should not sound like an AI summary, a literal translation, or a formal textbook.

---

## Core Goal

Turn long explanations into short notes that are:

- easy to scan
- easy to remember
- natural in Thai
- useful for review
- faithful to the original meaning
- concise without losing the main idea

The note should help the reader answer three questions fast:

- เรื่องนี้คืออะไร
- สำคัญตรงไหน
- ต้องจำอะไรบ้าง

---

## Working Process

Use this process in order.

### 1) Understand how Thai readers prefer to read in this situation

Before writing, decide the level and tone of Thai that fits the audience.

Think about:

- whether the reader is a university student
- whether the reader is reviewing before class, after class, or before an exam
- whether the topic is basic, intermediate, or advanced
- whether the note should sound casual, study-like, or exam-oriented

The language should feel familiar to Thai students. It should not sound like translated English. It should sound like normal Thai that people actually use when studying.

### 2) Read the original document and decide how to compress it

Do not shorten blindly.

First understand:

- the main topic
- the key subtopics
- the logic behind the explanation
- what is essential
- what can be removed without harming understanding

Then decide how the short note should be shaped.

A good short note is not a pile of fragments. It is a compact explanation with clear order.

### 3) Write the short note in a natural Thai style

Use simple Thai first.

Prefer words that students naturally recognize.

If a technical term is necessary, explain it in plain Thai immediately.

The note should sound like this kind of thinking:

- เข้าใจเรื่องนี้แบบสั้น ๆ
- จำประเด็นหลักให้ได้
- เห็นภาพรวมก่อน
- ทบทวนเร็วได้ทันที

### 4) Review and refine again

After drafting, read the note as if you are a Thai student seeing it for the first time.

Check whether it is:

- natural
- short enough
- clear enough
- faithful to the original meaning
- free from AI-like phrasing
- easy to review before an exam

Revise again if any sentence sounds forced, too formal, too mechanical, or too polished in a way real students would not normally write.

---

## Style Principles

### 1) Write like a real Thai student’s study note

The tone should be practical and familiar.

Good short notes often sound like this:

- สั้น
- ตรง
- อ่านแล้วเข้าใจเร็ว
- ไม่พยายามอธิบายเกินจำเป็น

Avoid writing that feels overly polished or artificially “perfect.”

### 2) Use Thai that fits the context

Thai language changes depending on situation and audience.

Choose wording that fits the study context, not a word-for-word translation.

For example, prefer wording that naturally appears in Thai notes, such as:

- คือ
- หมายถึง
- ใช้เพื่อ
- สำคัญตรงที่
- สรุปง่าย ๆ ว่า
- จำไว้ว่า

Do not force unnatural sentence structures just because the source text is in English or another language.

### 3) Keep meaning, but simplify form

The goal is not to copy the original structure.

The goal is to preserve the idea in a shorter, easier form.

This means:

- merge repeated ideas
- remove filler
- keep the central logic
- explain terms only when needed
- keep the note compact

### 4) Avoid AI-like decoration

Do not overuse:

- emojis
- decorative symbols
- excessive punctuation
- fancy formatting
- overly “neat” lines that look machine-made

The note should look human and practical.

A little structure is good. Too much decoration is not.

### 5) Use concise structure

The note should be easy to scan.

A useful short note often follows this pattern:

- หัวข้อหลัก
- ความหมายสั้น ๆ
- จุดสำคัญ
- ความต่างจากเรื่องอื่น
- สิ่งที่ต้องจำ

Keep the format stable across the document unless the content requires a different shape.

---

## Recommended Short Note Format

Use a simple structure like this when appropriate:

```md
## Topic Name

ความหมายสั้น ๆ

จุดสำคัญ:

- ...
- ...
- ...

สิ่งที่ควรจำ:

- ...
- ...

สรุป:
ประโยคสั้น ๆ ที่รวมใจความทั้งหมด
```
